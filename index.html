Diseases

Psoriasis

Psoriasis is a chronic skin condition characterized by red, itchy, and scaly patches. It is an autoimmune disease that often affects the skin, nails, and joints.

Causes:

The exact cause of psoriasis is not known, but it is believed to involve a combination of genetic and environmental factors.

Treatment:

Treatment options include topical creams, phototherapy, and systemic medications to manage symptoms and control inflammation.

Urticaria

Urticaria, commonly known as hives, is a skin rash that appears as raised, red, and itchy welts. It can be triggered by various factors, including allergies and stress.

Causes:

Allergies, medications, infections, and stress can contribute to the development of urticaria.

Treatment:

Antihistamines are often prescribed to relieve itching, and in severe cases, corticosteroids may be recommended.

Eczema

Eczema is a chronic skin condition that causes inflammation, itching, and redness. It can vary in severity and may be triggered by allergens or irritants.

Causes:

Genetics, environmental factors, and an overactive immune system are believed to contribute to the development of eczema.

Treatment:

Moisturizers, topical corticosteroids, and antihistamines are commonly used to manage symptoms and prevent flare-ups.

Lichen Planus

Lichen Planus is an inflammatory skin condition that can affect the skin, mucous membranes, and nails. It often appears as shiny, flat-topped bumps.

Causes:

The exact cause is unknown, but it is believed to involve an abnormal immune response.

Treatment:

Topical corticosteroids, antihistamines, and oral medications may be prescribed to alleviate symptoms.

Acne

Acne is a common skin condition that occurs when hair follicles become clogged with oil and dead skin cells. It often results in pimples, blackheads, and whiteheads.

Causes:

Excess oil production, clogged pores, bacteria, and hormonal changes are common factors contributing to acne.

Treatment:

Treatment options include topical retinoids, antibiotics, and oral contraceptives to manage acne lesions and prevent new ones from forming.

Vitiligo

Vitiligo is a skin disorder characterized by the loss of pigment in certain areas of the skin, resulting in white patches. The exact cause is not fully understood.

Causes:

The exact cause is unclear, but it is thought to involve an autoimmune response that attacks pigment-producing cells.

Treatment:

Treatment may include topical corticosteroids, phototherapy, and depigmentation for widespread vitiligo to even out skin tone.
